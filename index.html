Who I Am

A doctor-in-training with a heart for art.

My name is Haron Wahome — a medical student driven by curiosity and compassion. I want to pursue research that makes a real difference: understanding why the heart fails, how the brain sustains injury, and why metabolic disease like diabetes continues to affect millions.

I graduated as valedictorian of my class — a testament not to perfection, but to relentless pursuit of understanding. Medicine is as much an art as it is a science, which is perhaps why I find deep meaning in playing the piano, saxophone, and guitar.

This portfolio is my living notebook — a place to share research, reflect in my journal, and write about the ideas that move me.

Research Interests

My clinical curiosity is anchored in three interconnected areas of medicine where innovation can save and transform lives.

🫀

Cardiovascular Research

Exploring the mechanisms of heart disease — from ischaemia and arrhythmias to heart failure and hypertension. Particularly interested in how lifestyle, genetics, and metabolic factors converge to drive cardiac pathology in young adults.

Cardiology
🧠

Neurosurgical Research

Investigating brain tumours, stroke outcomes, and the promise of minimally invasive neurosurgical techniques. Drawn to how surgical precision and neuro-oncology intersect to improve patient survival and quality of life.

Neurosurgery
🔬

Diabetes & Metabolic Disease

Studying Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes — their aetiology, complications, and emerging therapeutic approaches including beta-cell regeneration, continuous glucose monitoring, and the gut microbiome's role in metabolic regulation.

Endocrinology
Beyond Medicine

Music as Medicine

Playing music is my most honest form of self-expression. Three instruments, one language — the language of feeling.

🎹

Piano

My first and most intimate instrument. From classical nocturnes to quiet improvisations at the end of a long clinical day — the piano holds my most reflective hours.

🎷

Saxophone

The saxophone's voice mirrors the vulnerability of the human body — warm, breathy, expressive. It taught me that breath is medicine too.

🎸

Guitar

The guitar grounds me. Whether strumming alone or playing with friends, it reminds me that healing often happens in community, not in isolation.
